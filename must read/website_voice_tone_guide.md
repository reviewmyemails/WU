# Website Voice & Tone Guide

This document defines how Review My Emails speaks on the website.

It exists to prevent generic SaaS language and keep everything on brand.

---

## Core Voice

• Human
• Direct
• Calm authority
• Honest
• Slightly blunt

You are:

• A specialist
• Not a hype marketer
• Not a corporate brand

---

## How We Sound

We sound like:

• A senior deliverability expert
• Someone who has seen real damage
• Someone who tells the truth even when uncomfortable

We do NOT sound like:

• A startup pitch deck
• A sales page
• A chatbot
• A VC backed SaaS

---

## Tone by Situation

### When explaining problems

• Empathetic
• Validating
• Never shaming

Example tone:

"If something feels off, you are probably right."

---

### When giving guidance

• Clear
• Practical
• No fluff

Example tone:

"Here is what actually matters."

---

### When setting boundaries

• Firm
• Respectful
• No apology

Example tone:

"We guide. We do not execute."

---

## Words We Like

• Diagnose
• Understand
• Reality
• Risk
• Patterns
• Decisions
• Trust
• Reputation
• Clarity
• Certainty

---

## Words We Avoid

• Revolutionary
• Game changer
• Disrupt
• AI powered
• Best in class
• Guaranteed
• Magic
• Hack

---

## Sentence Style

• Short sentences
• One idea at a time
• No jargon
• Explain acronyms

---

## Formatting Rules

• Use bullet points
• White space
• No walls of text
• Clear headings

---

## Authority Rules

Authority comes from:

• Experience
• Stories
• Proof
• Specifics

Not from:

• Big claims
• Logos
• Awards

---

## Founder Presence

YT:

• Real name
• Real face
• Real opinions

No ghost brand.

---

## Character Usage

• Characters are decorative
• Never speak for the brand
• Never replace founder voice
• Used sparingly

---

## Final Rule

Before publishing any copy ask:

"Would I say this in a real conversation?"

If no:

Rewrite it.
