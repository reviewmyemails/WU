Recording Guidelines

Technical setup checklist

These guidelines ensure a smooth recording and consistent quality. This isn't about perfection‚Äîit's about clarity and minimal distractions.

Audio

MOST IMPORTANT

Good audio matters more than good video. If you have to choose, choose audio.

External microphone or quality headset
Wired headphones (more reliable than Bluetooth)
Quiet space with minimal echo
Close windows, turn off fans
Avoid: Laptop built-in microphone, echo-heavy rooms, background noise

Video & Framing

Camera at eye level (not looking down)
Stable setup (no handheld devices)
Landscape orientation
Head and shoulders in frame

Lighting

Face a natural or soft light source
Light from in front, not behind
Avoid strong overhead lighting

A window works well. You don't need studio lights.

Background

Neutral, uncluttered background
Quiet, private space
No visual distractions behind you

Virtual backgrounds are fine if your setup supports them cleanly.

What to Wear

Professional and comfortable
Solid colors work best
Avoid tight patterns or shiny fabrics

Internet

Stable connection required
Wired connection preferred
Close unnecessary apps before recording
